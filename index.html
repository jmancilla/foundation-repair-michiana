Common Questions

Clear, professional explanations of structural issues, warning indicators, warranties, and estimate procedures.

What are the warning signs of a failing foundation?

Key warning indicators include stair-step cracks in exterior brickwork, horizontal or diagonal cracks in basement concrete walls, doors and windows sticking or refusing to close smoothly, uneven or sloping floors, and gaps between walls and ceilings.

Is the foundation warranty transferable to a new homeowner?

Yes. Our foundation stabilization piers and interior waterproofing drainage networks come with a lifetime transferable warranty. When selling your home, the warranty can be formally assigned to the buyer, securing the home's value.

How long does a typical waterproofing or piering project take?

Most basement waterproofing and crawl space encapsulation projects take 1 to 3 days to complete. Foundation piering projects stabilizing structural settlement typically take 3 to 5 days, depending on the number of piers required.

What causes basements in the Michiana region to leak?

Basement moisture is primarily driven by hydrostatic pressure. When the soils surrounding your foundation become saturated with water from heavy rain or snowmelt, the water exerts immense pressure against basement walls, forcing its way through micro-cracks and cold joints.
